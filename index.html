Learning outcomesRAG

Be able to use and manipulate surds, including rationalising the denominator
Be able to solve simultaneous equations in two variables by elimination and by substitution, including one linear and one quadratic equation
Be able to work with quadratic functions and their graphs, and the discriminant of a quadratic function, including the conditions for real and repeated roots
Be able to complete the square for a quadratic expression
Be able to solve quadratic equations including quadratic equations in a function of the unknown
Be able to solve linear and quadratic inequalities in a single variable and interpret such inequalities graphically
Be able to express solutions through correct use of "and" and "or", or through set notation including interval notation

Be able to simplify rational expressions including by factorising and division by linear expressions; divide a polynomial by a linear or quadratic expression

Be able to interpret the algebraic solution of equations graphically, and use intersection points to solve equations

Be able to decompose rational functions into partial fractions (with denominators no more complicated than repeated linear and irreducible quadratic factors)

Be able to use functions in modelling, including understanding limitations and refinements of models
1.03 — Coordinate Geometry
Learning outcomesRAG
Understand and be able to use the equation of a straight line, including the forms y = mx + c, y − y1 = m(x − x1), ax + by + c = 0
Be able to use the gradient conditions for two straight lines to be parallel or perpendicular
Be able to use straight line models in a variety of contexts
Understand and be able to use the coordinate geometry of a circle — the equation of a circle in standard form (x − a)² + (y − b)² = r²
Be able to use the following circle properties: the angle in a semicircle is 90°; the perpendicular from the centre bisects the chord; the radius at a tangent point is perpendicular to the tangent
Be able to use more complex contexts and applications of coordinate geometry including parametric equations of curves

Learning outcomesRAG

Understand and be able to work with sequences including those given by a formula for the nth term and those generated by a simple recurrence relation
Understand and be able to work with arithmetic sequences including the nth term and sum to n terms
Understand and be able to work with geometric sequences including the nth term, sum to n terms, and sum to infinity for |r| < 1
Be able to use sigma notation for sums of series
Understand and use the concept of a limit, and the sum to infinity of a geometric series
Be able to model situations using sequences and series

Learning outcomesRAG
Understand and be able to use the language of kinematics: position, displacement, distance, distance travelled, velocity, speed, acceleration
Understand, use and interpret graphs in kinematics for motion in a straight line: displacement-time, velocity-time, acceleration-time
Be able to interpret displacement-time and velocity-time graphs, and in particular understand the relationship between displacement, velocity and acceleration
Understand, use and derive the formulae for uniform acceleration (SUVAT equations): v = u + at, s = ut + ½at², v² = u² + 2as, s = ½(u + v)t

Learning outcomesRAG
Understand the concept and vector nature of a force; understand that forces can be represented as vectors and combined
Understand and be able to use Newton's first law: a particle remains at rest or in uniform motion unless acted on by a resultant force
Understand and be able to use Newton's second law: F = ma in the direction of motion, and for constant and variable forces

Understand and be able to use weight W = mg

Understand and be able to use Newton's third law: every action has an equal and opposite reaction
